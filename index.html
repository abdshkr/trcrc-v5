As many as 250 tree species can be found within 1 hectare of Malaysian rainforests.
Dipterocarps, scientifically known as Dipterocarpaceae, get their name from their two-winged fruit.
(di = two, pteron = wing and karpos = fruit)
Malaysia is listed in the top 12 megadiverse countries in the world - home to over 15,000 vascular plant species and 3,852 faunal species.
The world's tallest tropical tree can be found in Tawau, Sabah - with a towering height of 96.9 meters.

The Asian elephant is categorized as Endangered in the IUCN Red List since the year 1986. The population in Peninsular Malaysia was estimated at 1,450 in the year 2017.

Spanning over 260,000 hectares, the Kenyir Lake holds its title of largest man-made lake in South East Asia.

At Tropical Rainforest Conservation & Research Centre, we aim to play our part in protecting Malaysia's rare, threatened and endangered plant species. We believe in taking action and working on the ground in order to tackle biodiversity loss – one of the most pressing issues facing today’s society. Our conservation and landscape-level initiatives promote sustainable development and management practices.
